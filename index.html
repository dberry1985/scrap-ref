📘 Best Things to Scrap — How to Prep for Max Money

**No images** (per your preference). Tap a panel to expand.
General rules: Separate metals by grade, remove non‑metal attachments, keep everything as clean and sorted as possible, and always call local yards to confirm current rates & policies. Follow laws and yard safety instructions.
🔥 #1 Bare Bright Copper — highest-paying clean copper

What it is: Clean, uncoated, unalloyed copper wire (≥16 AWG), no insulation, no solder, no corrosion.

Where to find: Electrical upgrades, demo jobs, large gauge wire from appliances/AC installs.

Prep: Strip insulation; remove lugs/solder/fittings; sort by gauge; keep bright and dry.

Downgrades: Residual insulation, paint, heavy tarnish → #2/mixed.

Safety: Gloves/eye protection; never strip energized wire.

#1 Copper Wire & Tubing — clean copper pipes & large wire

Prep: Cut off fittings/valves; remove paint/solder where practical; keep only clean copper.

Downgrades: Soldered pipe, paint, mixed metals, significant corrosion.

Insulated Copper Wire (THHN/Romex) — sort by type/gauge

Prep: Sort THHN single conductors vs. Romex; bundle neatly; strip only if profitable.

Tip: Higher copper % gets better pricing.

Brass (Yellow & Red) — high value if clean & separated

Prep: Remove steel screws/inserts, rubber/plastic; separate yellow vs red brass.

Tip: Keep “dirty brass” (with steel) separate.

Electric Motors (copper wound)

Prep: Sell intact or tear down (remove steel cases, extract copper windings). Separate copper vs aluminum.

Safety: Unplug, discharge capacitors, PPE.

Transformers — copper core units beat aluminum

Prep: Identify copper vs aluminum windings; keep oil‑filled sealed & disclose.

Aluminum Wheels — clean, no tire, no weights

Prep: Remove tires/valves/weights; separate chrome‑clad vs clean cast.

Radiators — Cu/Brass, Al/Cu, or all‑Aluminum

Prep: Drain fluids; remove steel brackets/fans; sort by type.

Stainless 304

Prep: Magnet test; remove steel fasteners; avoid painted/laminated pieces.

Lead‑acid Batteries

Prep: Keep upright; don’t crack open; sort auto vs industrial where needed.

Safety: Acid hazard; PPE; transport per local rules.

Catalytic Converters

Prep: Only converters you legally own; pricing varies by model/year and PGM content. Use quote services.

Appliances / White Goods (steel)

Prep: Remove non‑metal parts; drain fluids; pull motors to sell separately; cut to “prepared” sizes if worth it.

Prepared Steel (P&S) vs Shred/Tin

Prep: Cut to yard size; separate heavy melt vs P&S vs light iron; remove non‑metal.

Tungsten Carbide (bonus)

Prep: Keep carbide tooling separate; verify yard buys it or find specialty buyers.

💡 Quick Best‑Practices

  • Separate by metal and grade; mixing high/low grades causes downgrades.
  • Remove steel/plastic/rubber/paint/solder where practical.
  • Bundle neatly; keep loads consistent by type (e.g., all THHN together).
  • Call 2–3 local yards for current prices; policies differ.
  • Follow yard rules, wear PPE, obey local/state regulations.
