📘 Best Things to Scrap — How to Prep for Max Money

**No images** (per your preference). Tap a panel to expand.
General rules: Separate metals by grade, remove non‑metal attachments, keep everything as clean and sorted as possible, and always call local yards to confirm current rates & policies. Never scrap items you don’t own; follow all applicable laws and yard safety instructions.
🔥 #1 Bare Bright Copper — highest-paying clean copper

What it is: Clean, uncoated, unalloyed copper wire (usually ≥16 AWG), no insulation, no solder, no corrosion.

Where to find: Electrical upgrades, demo jobs, old service panels, large gauge wire from appliances/AC installs.

How to prep:

  • Strip insulation completely (use a safe wire stripper). Keep the copper bright and untarnished.
  • Remove all attachments (lugs, soldered ends, tape). Keep only clean copper in this bin.
  • Sort by thickness—keep #1 bare bright separate from thinner mixed pieces.

Max payout tips: Keep it dry, coil neatly, avoid oxidation; yards pay **#1** only if it’s truly clean.

Common downgrades: Any residual insulation, paint/varnish, heavy tarnish → paid as lower grade (#2 or mixed).

Tools: Quality wire stripper, utility knife, side cutters, small bin for lugs/junk.

Safety: Wear gloves/eye protection; unplug power sources; never strip energized wire.

#1 Copper Wire & Tubing — clean copper pipes & large wire

What it is: Clean copper pipe/tubing free of solder/paint; large clean copper wire (still insulated → downgrade).

Where to find: Plumbing renovations, HVAC line sets (after removing fittings), water heater swaps.

How to prep:

  • Cut off fittings/valves; remove paint/solder where practical.
  • Drain and dry; avoid mixed loads (keep brass fittings separate).

Max payout tips: Deliver as **clean copper**—no steel screws, no soldered joints if possible.

Common downgrades: Soldered pipe, paint, mixed metal attachments, heavy corrosion.

Insulated Copper Wire (THHN/Romex) — separate by type for better rates

What it is: Building wire & household cable with insulation; payout depends on copper %.

Where to find: Electrical remodels, demo jobs, contractor offcuts.

How to prep:

  • Sort by type/gauge (THHN single conductors vs. Romex multi‑conductor).
  • Bundle neatly; don’t mix aluminum or low‑grade low‑copper wire with high‑grade bundles.
  • Strip only if it clearly pays more vs. time (call your yard for % thresholds).

Max payout tips: Higher copper content yields better pricing; keep loads consistent by type.

Common downgrades: Mixed low‑grade wire, excessive plastics, steel armor left attached.

Brass (Yellow & Red) — high value if clean & separated

What it is: Yellow (cartridge) brass & red brass (valves/fittings); value varies by alloy.

Where to find: Plumbing hardware, hose bibs, faucet bodies, radiator tanks (older units).

How to prep:

  • Remove steel screws, plastic inserts, rubber seats.
  • Sort yellow vs. red brass when you can (yards pay different rates).

Max payout tips: Deliver as clean solids; keep “dirty brass” (with steel) in a separate bin.

Common downgrades: Mixed metals attached, lacquer/paint, trapped non‑metal parts.

Electric Motors (copper wound) — steady payouts, better if disassembled

Where to find: HVAC compressors, power tools, appliances, treadmill motors.

How to prep:

  • Keep motors intact for quick sale; or tear down: remove steel cases, extract copper windings.
  • Separate aluminum vs. copper windings (copper pays more).

Max payout tips: Large copper‑wound motors pay the best; staged bins (steel, copper, aluminum) speed up yard grading.

Common downgrades: Mixed metals left attached, contamination (oil), aluminum windings assumed as lower value.

Safety: Unplug, discharge capacitors, wear gloves/eye protection.

Transformers — copper core units beat aluminum

Where to find: Old HVAC, UPS units, industrial panels.

Prep: Identify copper vs. aluminum windings; keep oil‑filled units sealed & disclose to yard.

Max payout tips: Separate copper‑core from aluminum‑core; avoid mixed loads.

Aluminum Wheels — clean, no tire, no weights

Prep: Remove tires, valve stems, wheel weights; separate chrome‑clad vs. clean cast.

Max payout tips: “Clean” wheels (no steel/lead) pay best; chrome‑clad usually pays less—keep separate.

Radiators — sort by metal: copper/brass, aluminum/copper, or all‑aluminum

Prep: Drain fluids; remove steel brackets/fans; sort by type.

Max payout tips: Cu/Brass radiators pay best; Al/Cu next; all‑aluminum lower. Keep each type in its own bin.

Stainless 304 — decent value if it’s really stainless

Where to find: Appliances, food/medical equipment, sinks.

Prep: Use a magnet (304 is non‑magnetic or weak); remove steel fasteners; avoid painted/laminated pieces.

Lead‑acid Batteries — steady pay; handle safely

Prep: Keep upright; don’t crack open; avoid leaks; sort auto vs. industrial where required.

Safety: Acid hazard—gloves/eye protection; transport per local rules; many yards pay by the unit or lb.

Catalytic Converters — PGM content varies widely; get quotes

Prep & policy: Only sell converters you legally own (from vehicles you own/parted). Prices depend on model/year & precious metals.

Tip: Use your existing app link for converter quotes (from the Copper page).

Appliances / White Goods — mostly steel (good for volume)

Prep: Remove non‑metal parts; drain fluids; separate motors (sell separately); prepared sizes may pay more vs. shred/tin.

Prepared Steel (P&S) vs. Shred/Tin — know your yard’s cut sizes

Prep: Cut to yard‑specified dimensions for “prepared” pricing; keep light iron/shred separate.

Max payout tips: Prepared usually beats shred; avoid mixed loads; remove non‑metal.

Tungsten Carbide (tooling) — high value, niche buyer

Where to find: Machine shops, drill bits, end mills.

Prep: Keep carbide separate; verify your yard buys it or find specialty buyers.

Aluminum Cans (UBC) — lightweight, consistent

Prep: Keep clean & dry; bag or bin; no steel/lead attachments; crushed is okay if yard accepts.

Tip: Volume matters—check if your yard pays more for bales or large loads.

💡 Quick Best‑Practices

  • Separate by metal and grade. Mixing high/low grades is the #1 reason for downgrades.
  • Remove steel, plastic, rubber, paint, solder wherever practical.
  • Bundle neatly; keep loads consistent (e.g., all THHN together, all Romex together).
  • Call 2–3 local yards for current prices; policies differ by yard & day.
  • Follow yard rules, wear PPE, and obey all local/state regulations.
